SUMMER CAMPS AT GOLF LOUNGE 18

This summer, have your junior golfers enjoy in structured programs from the comfort of perfect weather at Fairfield's premier indoor golf facility.

Half day camp (Mon-Fri) 9am to 12pm

Half day camps digs into detail with the golf game. Refining golf swings, reviewing course management.

These days will start with small welcome and explanation of our day, where we will lead to doing stretches that will be golf related, and then get into the schedule listed above.

  • Putting: Instructors will pay attention to the way they grip the club, the body’s position over the ball and the stroke.
  • Chipping: Instructors will look at grip, body’s position over the ball, the swing and the alignment
  • Irons/Drivers: Instructors will look at grip, swing, building consistency in the swing, also working on direction.
  • Course Management spend minimal but some time reviewing what course management is and explaining why and what the importance of course management is,

Weekly Camp (Mon-Fri) 2pm to 5pm

Week long camp that tackles and refines the basics of the golf swing and Game.

Each day we start with a light talk on what we will be working on for the day, Then do stretches to loosen up, and then start daily activities.

  • Monday: Putting and chipping (Run putting practice on simulator and allow the kids to putt, trying to get them to roll ball in straight line, then move to on course practice where we select a position around the green and make them chip to the pin)
  • Tuesday: Irons and Drivers (Set up the range and have the kids swing/warm-up, and have instructors take a look at all the kids swing and then help the kids with their swings. Then move to on course practice where we select tee shots and fairway shots that the kids will attempt to hit the greens)
  • Wednesday: Mini Games (Fun day for the kids, Longest Drive, closest to the pin, and putting contest)
  • Thursday: On course and rewards ceremony ( Set up the kids on a par 3 course where they can play 5-9 holes and have instructors help them along the way, the rewards will be from the previous day’s games along with the low score of on course play)
  • Friday: Introduce kids to light competition. Pair them up for a scramble tournament. Awards and Prizes given out at the end.
